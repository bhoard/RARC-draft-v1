Before you visit

Use this page style for the practical questions a visitor actually has: where to park, when to arrive, how field access works, and what kind of flying day they are walking into.

The article stays open on purpose. Plain headings, a few well-spaced paragraphs, and aligned photography do more here than wrapping every paragraph in another container.

Notice pattern: weather closures, event changes, and field maintenance updates can use this restrained treatment without becoming another heavy content box.
Same-day field status should be simple and honest.

If the runway is soft, the wind is high, or an event start time changes, this lighter note gives the page a believable update state without turning into an alert wall.

What to bring

Standard Gutenberg lists can carry AMA information, aircraft readiness, battery charging notes, and guest pilot expectations.

For longer stories, this page can alternate between plain text, paired photos, and one deliberate module rather than repeating the same box treatment section after section.

ArrivalPlan to arrive a little before the busiest flying window so a member can point out parking, pit area flow, and the flight line.
